Deploying TextiQ in Your Research Workflow

Transform Raw Text into Structured Insights

For academics, TextiQ transforms raw text from open-ended questions into structured, analysable insights. Imagine you've surveyed students on remote learning and have hundreds of text responses; this three-stage workflow shows you how to systematically analyse them.

Exploration & Discovery

This is your first pass to get a high-level overview. Deploy TextiQ immediately after data collection to let its AI automatically detect initial topics and analyse sentiment. This gives you an instant "pulse check" of your dataset, revealing the most frequent concepts and the general tone of the responses before you begin your detailed analysis.

Refinement & Structuring
🔧
▼
In this stage, you bring your researcher's intellect to the AI's output. Use TextiQ's tools to clean the data and refine the topics. Merge similar terms (e.g., 'lonely,' 'disconnected,' and 'isolated') into a single, robust theme like "Social Isolation". This transforms the raw text into a structured "codebook" of clear, analytically useful categories that will form the foundation of your findings.
↓
3
Reporting & Visualisation
📊
▼
Once your themes are defined, use TextiQ to communicate your findings. Instantly generate visuals like bar charts or word clouds to place directly into your manuscript or presentation. You can also easily filter responses to find the most powerful, illustrative quotes to support your arguments. This allows you to present compelling, data-driven evidence that strengthens your qualitative narrative with speed and precision.

Best Practices for Setting Up Topics

1
Start with Broad Themes
▼
Begin with general parent topics (like "Customer Service") and then create more specific sub-topics (like "Wait Time").
2
Name Topics Neutrally
▼
Use unbiased names like "Price" instead of "Expensive" to capture both positive and negative feedback on the same theme.
3
Let Feedback Guide You
▼
Before you build, check the automatic topic recommendations to see what your respondents are already talking about.
4
Include Synonyms
▼
Add different words people might use for the same idea (e.g., for "Cost," include "price," "fee," "value," etc.).
5
Check Untagged Responses
▼
Regularly review comments that don't fit into any topic, as they often reveal new or emerging issues.
6
Always Be Refining
▼
Your topic model is a living tool. Keep updating it as you collect more data to ensure it stays accurate and relevant.
