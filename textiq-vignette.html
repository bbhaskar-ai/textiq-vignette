Best Practices for Setting Up Topics

1
Start with Broad Themes
Begin with general parent topics (like "Customer Service") and then create more specific sub-topics (like "Wait Time").
2
Name Topics Neutrally
Use unbiased names like "Price" instead of "Expensive" to capture both positive and negative feedback on the same theme.
3
Let Feedback Guide You
Before you build, check the automatic topic recommendations to see what your respondents are already talking about.
4
Include Synonyms
Add different words people might use for the same idea (e.g., for "Cost," include "price," "fee," "value," etc.).
5
Check Untagged Responses
Regularly review comments that don't fit into any topic, as they often reveal new or emerging issues.
6
Always Be Refining
Your topic model is a living tool. Keep updating it as you collect more data to ensure it stays accurate and relevant.
